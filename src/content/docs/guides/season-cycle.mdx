A Hockay season is short and intense. The total cycle runs about seven weeks, which gives
roughly seven seasons per calendar year. The league never sleeps for long.

## Regular season—38 matchdays (~5.5 weeks)

Round-robin format: every team plays every other team twice (once home, once away) for
38 matchdays.

### Daily schedule

- **10 games per matchday** (all 20 teams play).
- First puck drop: **14:00 UITC** (also known as 2:00 PM UITC, but [The Ice thinks in absolute](/guides/faq#what-does-uitc-stand-for-again)).
- Games run sequentially, roughly 1 hour each including intermissions.
- The full slate runs from approximately 14:00 to 00:00 UITC—European fans get evening games, Asian fans get late-night, North American fans follow throughout the workday.

### Single-game structure (regular season)

- **Period 1**—~15 minutes of events
- **Intermission**—~5 minutes
- **Period 2**—~15 minutes of events
- **Intermission**—~5 minutes
- **Period 3**—~15 minutes of events
- **Post-game wrap**—~5 minutes (final stats, [JM](/lore/commentators) commentary)
- **Overtime / shootout** if tied (pushes subsequent games back—schedule drift is expected)

### Points

Two points for a win. One point for an overtime loss. Zero for a regulation loss.
[Le Council](/lore/lpchuip) trusts you can do the arithmetic.

## Playoffs—4 days

Eight teams qualify (top 8 from regular season standings). Single elimination.

**Bracket pairings are random.** No seeding. The Almighty Ice does not care about regular-season
accomplishments.

### Day 1—Quarterfinals

4 games. Regular one-hour format.

### Day 2—Semifinals

2 games. Regular one-hour format.

### Day 3—The Final

**One game. Special format.** Each period lasts **1 full hour** (~3–4 hours total with
intermissions). The only match in the season with this pacing.

[The Puck](/lore/the-puck) has more time here. Strange things happen.

After the Final, [The Puck speaks](/lore/the-puck#the-annual-sentence)—a single sentence.

### Day 4—Rest Day

No games. No announcements. No advisories. Silence. The only output is the final standings
and the Puck's sentence from Day 3. [Le Council recommends sitting with whatever just happened.](/guides/faq#what-is-rest-day)
