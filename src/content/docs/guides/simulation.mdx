Every match is broadcast in real time through [H.O.R.N.](/lore/organizations#horn--hockay-official-relay-network). Faceoffs, shots, saves, hits, penalties, goals, fights—announced as they occur. You experience the game as a live feed.
</Infobox>

## What the feed shows

The broadcast has enough granularity to follow flow and feel tension, not so granular that
it becomes noise. You will see:

- Faceoffs
- Shots on goal
- Saves
- Hits
- Penalties and power plays
- Goals
- Fights
- Period summaries

Between-period updates from the booth. A post-game wrap with final stats.

## Star ratings

Every player carries a public **1–5 star rating**, assessed by [Le Council's](/lore/lpchuip)
scouting department. [Modifiers](/modifiers/), arena effects, and the general unpredictability
of [The Almighty Ice](/lore/almighty-ice) mean a two-star player can outperform a five-star
player on any given day.

**The stars are a guide. The Almighty Ice is the authority.**

## What shapes a result

A game is not purely a matchup of ratings. Three layers of chaos sit on top of the simple
hockey rulebook:

1. [**Player modifiers**](/modifiers/player)—gained in [the Sixth](/lore/the-sixth). They travel with the player.
2. [**Arena modifiers**](/modifiers/arena)—one per home arena, always active there.
3. [**Match modifiers**](/modifiers/match)—announced before each game via a [LPCHUIP advisory](/modifiers/advisories).

When they combine, emergent chaos results. [Le Council](/lore/lpchuip) tracks all outcomes.
Le Council does not explain them.

## Fairness

The authority on whether a match was fair is The Almighty Ice. Le Council does not
adjudicate fairness. Le Council collects data and issues advisories.
