Hockay is a community-driven sports experience wrapped in an evolving mythology. Fans watch, bet, draft, vote, and
collectively shape the fate of a world governed by forces they don't fully understand.

The rules are hockey-like and deliberately simple. Pucks go in nets. There are three
periods. You can fight. No complicated rules. The complexity lives in the mythology,
the [modifiers](/modifiers/), and the community—not the rulebook.

## How to pronounce it

It is pronounced **"Hockay"** or **"Hoqué"**. Not "Hockey." Not "Hock-ee." Not "Okay."
[The Almighty Ice](/lore/almighty-ice) was very clear about this.

## The essentials

- **20 teams** across six continents. [See them all](/teams/).
- **15 players per team**, 500+ players total. Each has hidden stats and a 1–5 star public rating.
- **Season** of ~7 weeks: 38 matchdays of regular season, 4-day playoffs. [Full cycle →](/guides/season-cycle)
- **Currency**: Looneys. Soft currency only, no real money. [More →](/guides/economy)
- **Governing body**: [Le Permanent Council du Hockay](/lore/lpchuip) (LPCHUIP). Half sports league, half paranormal investigation unit.

## What makes it Hockay

Three layered systems of modifiers make every match something other than plain hockey:

1. [**Player modifiers**](/modifiers/player)—gained or lost in [The Sixth](/lore/the-sixth).
2. [**Match modifiers**](/modifiers/match)—announced before each game as [LPCHUIP advisories](/modifiers/advisories).
3. [**Arena modifiers**](/modifiers/arena)—one per team's home building. Always active at home.

And behind all three: [The Almighty Ice](/lore/almighty-ice).

## What do I actually do?

You watch. You bet. You pick a [team](/teams/) and care about them more than is reasonable.
You check scores at work. You argue with strangers about whether [Displacement](/modifiers/arena)
is actually a disadvantage. You refresh the feed during a [Whiteout](/modifiers/arena) and
see nothing, because that is the point. Hockay is a spectator sport. You are the spectator.
[The Almighty Ice](/lore/almighty-ice) is the sport.
