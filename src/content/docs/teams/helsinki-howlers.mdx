## The arena—lore

The Dark Sauna is, architecturally, a sauna. A very large sauna that happens to contain a regulation ice rink. The Almighty Ice appeared inside an existing public bathhouse complex, and the building grew around it overnight. The steam is real—it seeps from vents in the floor that no plumber can trace, thickening the air above The Almighty Ice in a persistent low haze. Visiting teams complain that they can't see the far boards. Home fans consider this an advantage. The Howlers have played in partial darkness since their first game. They don't seem to mind. Le Council has measured the noise levels during Howlers home games and classified the data. The seismograph readings from Helsinki Geophysical Observatory correlate with the crowd at The Dark Sauna on game nights.

## The arena—building

Clad in pale birch wood and dark stone, The Dark Sauna looks from the outside like a Finnish bathhouse scaled to absurdity. The roofline is low and curved, following the contour of the land. Steam vents along the eaves release thin trails of vapor into the Helsinki air year-round. The entrance is through a series of progressively warmer corridors—the lobby is cool, the concourse is warm, and by the time you reach the seating bowl, the air is thick and humid despite The Almighty Ice below. The seats are dark wood benches, heated from beneath. The lighting is deliberately dim—warm amber downlights that leave the upper reaches of the building in shadow. The Almighty Ice glows in the darkness. The scoreboard is backlit, not bright. Everything is designed to absorb sound and redirect it downward, creating a resonant chamber where the crowd's noise becomes physical. Opposing teams describe it as playing inside a drum.
