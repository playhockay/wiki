<Infobox variant="sixth" title="What it is">
The Sixth is the sixth dimension. Players sent to the Sixth are exposed to whatever's on the other side. When they return, they may have gained or lost a modifier.
</Infobox>

During any game, there is a small but non-zero probability that a player entering the
penalty box will briefly cease to exist on the ice surface. They reappear eventually —
usually within the same game, occasionally not. No returning player has provided a
coherent account.

## What happens when a player returns

Players may return:

- [**Luminous**](/modifiers/player#luminous), [**Sharp**](/modifiers/player#sharp), [**Awake**](/modifiers/player#awake)—the positive modifiers.
- [**Foggy**](/modifiers/player#foggy), [**Brittle**](/modifiers/player#brittle), [**Hollow**](/modifiers/player#hollow)—the negative ones.
- Speaking languages no one recognizes ([Translated](/modifiers/player#translated)).
- With more gravity than they left with.
- Hearing things before they happen ([Echo](/modifiers/player#echo)).
- Moving with something the rest of us can't feel ([Tidal](/modifiers/player#tidal)).

A star player enters with Luminous and might return without it—or worse, with something
else entirely. This makes penalty avoidance a genuine strategic concern.

## The gamble

The Sixth is a gamble, not a power-up machine. Modifiers stack over a career, creating
deeply weird veteran players who become legends. Early seasons have simple, mostly
positive modifiers. Later seasons introduce stranger, more double-edged ones.

## The leaking

Over many seasons, the Sixth starts leaking. More modifiers. Weirder ones. Players
returning with knowledge they shouldn't have.

[Le Council](/lore/lpchuip) classifies this as an "Unexplained Ice Phenomenon" and
assures you that concern is understandable but misplaced.

## Sample broadcast announcements

> "Kowalski has returned from the Sixth. She is no longer Luminous."

> "Kowalski has returned from the Sixth. She seems... taller."
