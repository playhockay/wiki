</Infobox>

Before certain games, conditions are imposed on the match itself. Le Council names the
condition and issues an advisory. The community reads between the lines.

## Catalogued conditions

### Thin Ice
> Skating reduced for all players.

Slower game. More turnovers. More falls.

### Mercury
> Losing team gets a cumulative advantage as the deficit grows.

Comebacks more likely. Third-period comebacks under Mercury are the most-watched moments
in the league.

### Fog
> Goalies see less.

Higher scoring. A favorite for bettors who like the over.

### Double or Nothing
> Standings points and bet payouts double.

Everything matters more. Advisories under Double or Nothing often include a Le Council
disclaimer about not being responsible for outcomes.

### Clean Ice
> No modifier. Announced as a modifier.

Nothing unusual will happen. Supposedly. The community will never fully believe nothing
is happening.

## Other conditions

Other match conditions have been documented but not officially named by Le Council.
Community records note, among others:

- Games in which one event appears to have been retroactively changed. See [The Jambono](/lore/the-jambono).
- Games in which [player modifiers](/modifiers/player) feel more pronounced than usual.
- Games that behave normally until they don't.

[Le Council](/lore/lpchuip) has acknowledged several of these patterns without naming them.
[The rest is Glacified.](/lore/glacified)
