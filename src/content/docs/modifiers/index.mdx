Hockay inherits a very simple rulebook from ice hockey and then stacks three layers of
chaos on top. Those layers are called modifiers.

When a shot becomes a goal, when a save is impossible, when a player skates left instead
of right, the explanation is almost always a modifier. The simulation produces outcomes;
the community builds the explanations.

## The three tiers

1. [**Player modifiers**](/modifiers/player)—gained, lost, or swapped in [The Sixth](/lore/the-sixth). They stay with a player until something changes them.
2. [**Match modifiers**](/modifiers/match)—announced before each game by [LPCHUIP advisory](/modifiers/advisories). One per game, occasionally zero (but "zero" is itself a modifier called **Clean Ice**).
3. [**Arena modifiers**](/modifiers/arena)—semi-permanent traits tied to each team's home building. Known, documented, part of team identity.

## How they combine

Modifiers stack, and they interact. When a Luminous player visits The Frozen Dock during
a Whiteout, nobody—including [Le Council](/lore/lpchuip)—can confidently predict what
will happen. That is the point.

Some interactions are documented. Most are not. The community maps them collectively.
What the community cannot yet map remains [Glacified](/lore/glacified).

## Resolution

[Le Council](/lore/lpchuip) has not published the order in which modifiers resolve. Community
theories exist. [Le Council declines to confirm any of them.](/lore/glacified)
