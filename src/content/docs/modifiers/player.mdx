<Infobox variant="sixth" title="Where they come from">
Player modifiers are gained, lost, or swapped by time spent in [The Sixth](/lore/the-sixth) (the penalty box). The Sixth is not a power-up machine. Whether a modifier helps or hurts depends on context. Whether it fades depends on The Ice.
</Infobox>

Le Council has so far identified the following modifiers, grouped into four broad
categories. The list will grow as Le Council's investigators catalogue more. Modifier
effects are often context-dependent; what helps one player may hurt another.

## Positive

### Luminous
> "They glow differently now."

### Awake
> "Something opened their eyes in [the Sixth](/lore/the-sixth)."

### Echo
> "They hear things before they happen."

### Sharp
> "Everything they do has an edge now."

## Negative

### Foggy
> "They can't quite see straight."

### Brittle
> "They came back thinner somehow."

### Wandering
> "They keep skating to the wrong places."

## Ambiguous

### Magnetic
> "[The Puck](/lore/the-puck) finds them."

High event involvement. More shots, more hits, more penalties. Great for a star.
Terrible for a liability.

### Doubled
> "There seems to be two of them."

A random event per game counts twice—a goal scores twice, a penalty is called twice.
Which event, nobody controls.

### Ancient
> "They remember things from before [The Almighty Ice](/lore/almighty-ice)."

## Weird ([Glacified](/lore/glacified))

### Translated
> "They came back speaking a language no one recognizes."

Effects are Glacified.

### Hollow
> "Something stayed behind in the Sixth."

Effects are Glacified.

### Tidal
> "They move with something the rest of us can't feel."

Effects are Glacified. Appear to be period-dependent.

## Career accumulation

Modifiers stack over a career, creating deeply weird veteran players who become legends.
[Le Council](/lore/lpchuip) tracks all outcomes. Le Council does not explain them.
