<Infobox variant="neutral" title="Why this exists">
[Le Council](/lore/lpchuip) does not control match conditions. It either observes them or receives notifications from [The Almighty Ice](/lore/almighty-ice). Le Council is a middleman trying to interpret something it doesn't fully understand. Weather forecasters for a god.
</Infobox>

Every [match modifier](/modifiers/match) reaches the public via a pre-game LPCHUIP
advisory. Advisories come in three delivery tiers, each with different implications.

## The three tiers

### Tier 1—The Almighty Ice notifies directly (ominous)

The Ice tells Le Council. Le Council relays. This is the most common tier and the most
reassuring—the Ice is communicating.

> "LPCHUIP MATCH ADVISORY: The Almighty Ice has notified Le Council that tonight's match
> at The Sandy Parlor will be played under **Wishes** conditions. Le Council did not request
> this information."

### Tier 2—Le Council observes (normal)

Le Council's instruments detect the conditions. The Ice has not notified anyone. Le Council
is guessing, politely.

> "LPCHUIP MATCH ADVISORY: Based on current observations of The Almighty Ice, tonight's
> conditions at The Frozen Dock are expected to be **Whiteout**. This forecast is provided
> as-is. Le Council makes no guarantees regarding accuracy or completeness."

### Tier 3—Silence from The Almighty Ice (unsettling)

No notification. No observable signal. Le Council presumes default conditions. The
community watches more closely during these games.

> "LPCHUIP MATCH ADVISORY: The Almighty Ice has not communicated with Le Council regarding
> tonight's match at The Oldest Rink. Conditions are presumed to be **Clean Ice**. Le Council
> finds the silence unusual but not unprecedented."
